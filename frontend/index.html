How It Works

A consensus-based adaptive sampling algorithm with triple stopping criteria

START — New Question
↓
Generate 2 Samples (temperature sampling)
↓
Extract Numerical Answer from Each Response
↓
Compute Agreement: majority_count / total_samples
↓
Triple Gate Check
total ≥ 4 AND majority ≥ 3 AND ratio ≥ θ
↓
ALL MET
↓
STOP & Return Answer
Confident — save compute
NOT ALL MET
↓
Sample 2 More
Not confident — spend more
↑ repeat until budget (8 max)

About This Research

The Problem

Current LLMs spend the same inference compute on every query regardless of difficulty. Easy questions waste resources; hard questions may not get enough reasoning effort. As of 2026, papers confirm that "no single test-time strategy is optimal across all inputs".

Our Approach

We build a lightweight controller that monitors answer agreement across multiple LLM samples and dynamically stops when a triple criterion is met: minimum samples, minimum agreement count, and minimum confidence ratio. The result is a simple, interpretable, and reproducible method for adaptive test-time compute allocation.

Research Context

Test-time compute scaling is an active 2025–2026 research direction. While partial solutions exist (heuristic early-exit, speculative decoding), a clean general stopping policy with formal guarantees remains an open problem. This project contributes to that gap.
